Novi, MI - A Welcoming Community for Businesses and People

Less than 30 miles outside of Detroit you will find Novi, a cultural and economic hub in Oakland County. Researchers have taken notice of what a great place Novi is. Safety makes all the difference in an area where you live and work - a reason Novi is proud to have been named the 25th safest city in Michigan according to Alarms.org. Money Magazine ranked Novi as the 23rd best city in America and the best city in Michigan to live for its low unemployment (1.8% in 2017), shopping options, high average income, and family-friendly amenities.

Novi has been welcoming a diverse group of residents, visitors, and businesses since before it became a city in 1969. With more than 60 companies based in Japan having locations in Novi including Toyota Boshoku America and Japanese schools, it is clear to see why Owani, Japan considers Novi to be a sister city. Additionally, other car retailers or manufacturers, energy companies, and healthcare organizations such as St. John’s Providence Health System call Novi home.

In addition to the top-ranked public school district and 95% graduation rate, Novi is the proud home of Walsh College’s technology-enriched campus. The sprawling college property includes 35,000 square foot of classrooms, open computer labs, a library, bookstore, student lounge, and meeting space provides a complete educational experience for residents of Oakland and Wayne counties. Find Walsh college’s Novi campus just off I-96 and Meadowbrook Road.

Look no further than Novi for both indoor and outdoor things to do. Go for a swim in the nearby Walled Lake than when the leaves begin to turn, stop by Yates Cider Mill just outside the city for delicious treats. Head inside of Twelve Oaks Mall to browse over 150 stores. The town also hosts an annual event known as The Main Event Novi in early August.
